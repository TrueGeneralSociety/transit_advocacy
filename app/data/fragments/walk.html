History

Walking is one of the oldest and most fundamental modes of human transportation, dating back to prehistoric times. Early humans relied on walking for survival, using it to hunt, gather food, and explore their surroundings.

Importance of Walking

Walking is a crucial aspect of daily life, providing numerous health benefits and serving as a sustainable mode of transportation. It promotes physical fitness, reduces the risk of chronic diseases, and contributes to mental well- being. Additionally, walking is an eco-friendly alternative to motorized transportation, helping to reduce carbon emissions and traffic congestion.

Our Perspective

Regardless of what distance you are traveling, your journey begins and ends with walking.

We view walking as more than just a means of getting from one place to another; it is a way to connect with our environment and community. Walking allows us to experience the world at a human pace, fostering a deeper appreciation for our surroundings and encouraging social interactions. From leisurely strolls in the park to purposeful walks through bustling city streets, walking enriches our lives in countless ways.

Concerns

Our concerns with walking primarily revolve around safety and accessibility. While walking is a simple and natural activity, many urban environments pose challenges for pedestrians. Inadequate sidewalks, poor lighting, and traffic hazards can make walking unsafe, particularly for vulnerable populations such as children, the elderly, and individuals with disabilities.

Additionally, urban sprawl and car-centric infrastructure often prioritize motorized transportation over pedestrian needs, leading to environments that are not conducive to walking. This can discourage people from choosing to walk as a mode of transportation, limiting its potential benefits.

The way forward

To promote walking as a safe and accessible mode of transportation, cities should prioritize pedestrian-friendly infrastructure and urban design. This includes investing in well-maintained sidewalks, crosswalks, and lighting, implementing traffic calming measures, and creating walkable neighborhoods that encourage pedestrian activity. Additionally, public awareness campaigns can help educate communities about the benefits of walking and the importance of pedestrian safety.
