History

The automobile was invented in the late 19th century and quickly became a popular mode of transportation. Over the years, cars have evolved significantly, with advancements in technology leading to improved safety, performance, and fuel efficiency.

Cars are a common mode of transportation, offering convenience and flexibility for short to medium distances. However, they contribute to traffic congestion and environmental pollution.

Our Perspective

Let's make one thing clear: cars are not just a means of transportation; they are a symbol of freedom, status, and personal expression to many. From the sleek curves of a sports car to the rugged durability of an SUV, cars reflect our personalities and lifestyles.

Our qualms are not with cars themselves but with how we use them and the infrastructure centered around them. Urban sprawl, traffic congestion, and pollution are all byproducts of a car-centric society. The challenge lies in balancing our love for cars with sustainable urban planning and environmental stewardship.

In fact, any car lover who understands the urban impacts of car-centric infrastructure would agree that promoting alternative transportation methods is essential for creating healthier, more livable cities. And more importantly, it gives them more space to drive and enjoy their vehicles.
